## Alert Levels

| Risk Score | Level    | Colour |
|------------|----------|--------|
| ≥ 65%      | HIGH     | Red    |
| 35–64%     | MODERATE | Amber  |
| < 35%      | LOW      | Green  |

---

## District Health Officer — Response Protocols

### HIGH Alert
1. Activate the District Rapid Response Team (RRT) within 24 hours.
2. Issue a public health advisory via official district channels.
3. Deploy mobile fever surveillance units to the top 3 high-burden sub-districts.
4. Requisition NS1 antigen rapid test kits (minimum 500 units) from the state warehouse.
5. Coordinate with the District Malaria Officer for joint vector-control operations.
6. Submit a District Situation Report to the State Health Directorate within 48 hours.

### MODERATE Alert
1. Place RRT on standby — 6-hour activation readiness.
2. Increase passive surveillance frequency at all PHCs to daily reporting.
3. Conduct targeted fogging and larviciding in wards with reported cases.

### LOW Alert
1. Maintain routine weekly surveillance.
2. Ensure PHC stock of ORS and paracetamol is above minimum threshold.

## Hospital Manager — Operational Protocols

### HIGH Alert
1. Activate Dengue Surge Plan — open dedicated dengue ward if not already active.
2. Target minimum 20 dengue-designated beds; escalate to CMO if capacity is insufficient.
3. Ensure 5-day buffer stock: IV Normal Saline (500 ml bags), IV Dextrose, platelets.
4. Place blood bank on 24-hour standby for platelet transfusion requests.
5. Brief all triage nurses on NS1 rapid testing protocol.
6. Report bed occupancy to District Health Officer daily.

### MODERATE Alert
1. Review current dengue bed allocation — adjust if occupancy exceeds 60%.
2. Order a 3-day top-up of NS1 rapid test kits and IV fluids.
3. Ensure haematology lab is processing CBC and platelet counts same-day.

### LOW Alert
1. Maintain routine dengue case management protocols.
2. Verify NS1 kit expiry dates during weekly pharmacy audit.

## State Official — Strategic Response

### State-Wide HIGH (≥ 3 districts at HIGH alert)
1. Convene the State Emergency Operations Committee (SEOC) within 48 hours.
2. Authorise inter-district transfer of NS1 kits and IV fluid buffer stocks from state reserves.
3. Deploy a State Rapid Response Team to the highest-risk district.
4. Issue a state-level press release; coordinate with media cell.
5. Request central support if active alerts exceed 5 districts simultaneously.

### Targeted District Action
1. Prioritise resource deployment to districts with risk_score > 65%.
2. Districts between 35–65%: increase reporting frequency; no state-level resource deployment yet.
3. Review aggregate predicted cases weekly and update state forecast dashboard.

## Vector Control
- Fogging: Only indicated when adult Aedes density index > 1. Coordinate with municipality.
- Larviciding: Mandatory in all water-storage sites within 200 m of reported cases.
- Source reduction: Weekly campaigns in high-density residential zones during outbreak weeks.

## Escalation Contacts
- District → State: Submit through the IDSP portal within 24 hours of HIGH alert.
- State → Centre: Use IHIP platform; copy Director of National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme.
